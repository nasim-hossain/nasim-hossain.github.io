About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering at New Jersey Institute of Technology, developing advanced computational methods to understand blood flow mechanics in growing microvascular networks. My research combines high-fidelity CFD simulations, multiphysics modeling, and high-performance computing to reveal how mechanical forces drive vascular growthâ€”with applications in cancer biology, wound healing, and cardiovascular disease.

Over 6+ years, I have built expertise that spans both academic research and industrial R&D. My doctoral research, worked in $500K+ federal research funding (NSF & NIH), has produced 5 peer-reviewed publications (3 first-author) in top journals, 10+ conference presentations and posters, and national media coverage. Before graduate school, I earned a B.Sc. in Aeronautical Engineering and worked as an R&D Design Engineer, leading the development of products including a commercial FDM 3D printing system.
