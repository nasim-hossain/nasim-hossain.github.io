About Me

I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the New Jersey Institute of Technology with over 6 years of combined academic and industry experience in microvascular hemodynamics, high-fidelity computational fluid dynamics (CFD), and multiphysics simulations. My research specializes in 3D red blood cell (RBC) resolved simulations of complex microvascular geometries using an in-house solver that integrates finite volume, spectral, and finite element methods.

I have developed project-specific modules that extend solver capabilities to meet targeted research objectives, contributing to a $500k NSF-funded project. My work has resulted in high-impact peer-reviewed journal publications, conference presentations at top-tier venues, and national media coverage.
